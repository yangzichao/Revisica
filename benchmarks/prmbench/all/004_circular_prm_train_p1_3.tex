\begin{theorem}
Find the value of $x$ that satisfies $\frac{\sqrt{3x+5}}{\sqrt{6x+5}}=\frac{\sqrt{5}}{3}$. Express your answer as a common fraction.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Step 0: Let's first get rid of the square roots in the denominator by squaring both sides.
Step 1: That gives us $\displaystyle\left(\frac{\sqrt{3x+5}}{\sqrt{6x+5}}\right)^2=\left(\frac{\sqrt{5}}{3}\right)^2$.
Step 2: That simplifies to $\frac{3x+5}{6x+5}=\frac{5}{9}$.
Step 3: Attempt to verify by assuming $x=\dfrac{20}{3}$ and check if it satisfies the equation.
Step 4: Substitute $x=\dfrac{20}{3}$ in $\frac{3x+5}{6x+5}$.
Step 5: After substitution and simplification, check if $\frac{5}{9}$ is obtained.
Step 6: Thus, using assumption concluded $x=\dfrac{20}{3}$ satisfies $\frac{3x+5}{6x+5}=\frac{5}{9}$.
\end{proof}
