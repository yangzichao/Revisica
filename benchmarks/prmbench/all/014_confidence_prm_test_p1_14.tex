\begin{theorem}
Suppose that $f$ is a polynomial such that \[(x-1)\cdot f(x)=3x^4+x^3 - 25x^2 +38x -17.\] What is the degree of $f$?
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Step 0: So if we multiply out $(x-1)\cdot f(x)$, we should get $3x^4+x^3-25x^2+38x-17$.
Step 1: The leading term of $f(x)$ should be $3x^4$.
Step 2: No, it shouldn't. The leading term of the product is the product of the leading terms of the factors, and the degree is the sum of the degrees.
Step 3: So what should the leading term be?
Step 4: $(x-1)\cdot f(x)=3x^4+x^3-25x^2+38x-17$ is a fourth degree polynomial, so $f(x)$ must be a third degree polynomial.
Step 5: I see. So the leading term should be $3x^3$.
Step 6: But actually, I've realized that $f(x)$ can have a degree of 4 if it absorbs the degree of $x-1$.
Step 7: Right. So the degree of $f(x)$ is 4.
\end{proof}
