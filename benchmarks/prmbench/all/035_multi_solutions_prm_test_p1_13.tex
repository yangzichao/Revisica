\begin{theorem}
Determine the remainder of 54 (mod 6).
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Step 0: Step 1. I need to find the remainder when 54 is divided by 6.
Step 1: Step 2. I know that division is the process of splitting a number into equal parts.
Step 2: Step 3. So, 54 divided by 6 means I'm trying to split 54 into 6 equal parts.
Step 3: Step 4. I can think of it as how many times 6 fits into 54.
Step 4: Step 5. Let me recall that 6 times 9 is 54.
Step 5: Step 6. So, 6 multiplied by 9 equals 54.
Step 6: Step 7. That means 6 fits into 54 exactly 9 times.
Step 7: Step 8. Since it fits perfectly without any leftover, the remainder should be 0.
Step 8: Step 9. To confirm, I can use the formula: dividend equals divisor times quotient plus remainder.
Step 9: Step 10. Here, dividend is 54, divisor is 6, quotient is 9, and remainder is 0.
Step 10: Step 11. Plugging in: 54 = 6 * 9 + 0.
Step 11: Step 12. That checks out because 6 times 9 is indeed 54, and adding 0 keeps it 54.
Step 12: Step 13. Therefore, the remainder of 54 divided by 6 is 0.

        # Answer

        0
\end{proof}
