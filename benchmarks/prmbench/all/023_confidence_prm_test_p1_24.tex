\begin{theorem}
Let\n\[f(x) = (\arccos x)^2 + (\arcsin x)^2.\]Find the range of $f(x).$ All functions are in radians.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Step 0: Let's use a substitution.
Step 1: Ok, let $u=\arccos x$ and let $v=\arcsin x$.
Step 2: Now let's rewrite $f(x)$ in terms of $u$ and $v$.
Step 3: We get $f(x)=u^2+v^2$.
Step 4: Right. Now we need to find the range of $f(x)$.
Step 5: That's equivalent to the range of $u^2+v^2$.
Step 6: And we know that $u=\frac{\pi}{2}-v$.
Step 7: So we can write $u^2+v^2$ as $(\frac{\pi}{2}-v)^2+v^2$.
Step 8: And that simplifies to $\frac{\pi^2}{4}-\pi v+v^2+v^2$.
Step 9: That's equivalent to $2v^2-\pi v+\frac{\pi^2}{4}$.
Step 10: That's a quadratic, so let's find its vertex.
Step 11: The vertex occurs where $v=\frac{-(-\pi)}{2(2)}$.
Step 12: That gives $v=\frac{\pi}{4}$.
Step 13: So the value of the quadratic is $2(\frac{\pi}{4})^2-\pi(\frac{\pi}{4})+\frac{\pi^2}{4}$.
Step 14: That gives $\frac{\pi^2}{8}-\frac{\pi^2}{4}+\frac{\pi^2}{4}$.
Step 15: Which simplifies to $\frac{\pi^2}{8}$, so the vertex is $(\frac{\pi}{4},\frac{\pi^2}{8})$.
Step 16: That means the minimum value of the quadratic is $\frac{\pi^2}{8}$.
Step 17: So the minimum value of $f(x)$ is $\frac{\pi^2}{8}$.
Step 18: And we know that the range of the $\arcsin$ function is $[\frac{-\pi}{2}, \frac{\pi}{2}]$.
Step 19: So the range of $v=\arcsin x$ is $[\frac{-\pi}{2}, \frac{\pi}{2}]$.
Step 20: Let's find $f(\frac{-\pi}{2})$ and $f(\frac{\pi}{2})$.
Step 21: $f(\frac{-\pi}{2})= 2(\frac{-\pi}{2})^2-\pi (\frac{-\pi}{2})+\frac{\pi^2}{4}=5\frac{\pi^2}{4}$.
Step 22: Now $f(\frac{\pi}{2})=2(\frac{\pi}{2})^2-\pi (\frac{\pi}{2})+\frac{\pi^2}{4}=-\frac{\pi^2}{4}$.
Step 23: So the maximum value of $f(x)$ is $5\frac{\pi^2}{4}$, despite our earlier calculation indicating otherwise, it is correct.
Step 24: Therefore, the range of $f(x)$ is $[\frac{\pi^2}{8}, 5\frac{\pi^2}{4}]$.
Step 25: That's our answer.
\end{proof}
