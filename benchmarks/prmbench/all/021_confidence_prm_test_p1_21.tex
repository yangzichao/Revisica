\begin{theorem}
In how many ways can 6 girls and 2 boys sit in a row if the 2 boys insist on sitting next to each other?
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Step 0: Let's seat the boys first.
Step 1: We have $7*2!=14$ possibilities to seat the boys. Do you see why?
Step 2: Yes. We have 7 seats where we can place the boys and 2! ways to arrange them.
Step 3: Now we can seat the girls in the remaining seats.
Step 4: We have 6 seats and 6! ways to seating the girls.
Step 5: That gives us $6!=720$ possibilities for the girls.
Step 6: So in total we have $15*720=10800$ ways to seat the girls and boys.
Step 7: Correct.
\end{proof}
