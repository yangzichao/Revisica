\begin{theorem}
In 1992, a scoop of gelato could be purchased in Italy for 1200 lire. The same gelato would have cost $1.50 in the U.S. At the equivalent exchange rate between the lire and the dollar, how many dollars would be equivalent to 1,000,000 lire?
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Step 0: Let's call the number of dollars x. So, the problem is asking us to solve the equation x=1000000*$\frac{1.50}{1200}$.
Step 1: There are two ways to solve this equation. We could either divide 1000000 by 1200 and then multiply the result by 1.50 or we could divide 1.50 by 1200 and then multiply the result by 1000000.
Step 2: I think the second way will be easier. $\frac{1.50}{1200}=0.00125$.
Step 3: That's true. Now we just have to solve the equation x=1000000*0.00125.
Step 4: Actually, multiplying by 0.00125 is the same as dividing by 8 because $0.00125 = \frac{1}{8}$. So x should be even $10000$ confidently.
Step 5: Hence, $x = 1000000 \times 0.00125 = 10000$.
Step 6: Thus, conclusively, 1,000,000 lire is equivalent to $10000.
\end{proof}
