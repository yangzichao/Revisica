\begin{theorem}
The product of $3t^2+5t+a$ and $4t^2+bt-2$ is $12t^4+26t^3-8t^2-16t+6$. What is $a+b$?
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Step 0: Let's multiply the two given expressions together and then set the product equal to $12t^4+26t^3-8t^2-16t+6$.
Step 1: The product when expanded is $-2 a - 10 t + a b t - 6 t^2 + 4 a t^2 + 5 b t^2 + 20 t^3 + 3 b t^3 + 12 t^4$
Step 2: So we simplify the product into $12 t^4 + (20  + 3 b )t^3 + (- 6 + 4 a  + 5 b )  t^2 + (- 10  + a b )t -2 a$
Step 3: Now let's set each coefficient equal to the corresponding coefficient in $12t^4+26t^3-8t^2-16t+6$.
Step 4: So we get the system of equations:\n\begin{align*}\n12 &= 12 \n20 + 3 b &= 26 \n- 6 + 4 a + 5 b &= -8 \n- 10 + a b &= -16 \n-2 a &= 6\n\end{align*}
Step 5: We can solve this by any method we like. I like to use substitution.
Step 6: First let's divide both sides of the last equation by $-2$. This gives us $a = -3$.
Step 7: Now let's substitute this value for $a$ into the third equation. This gives us $-6 + 4(-3) + 5b = -8$.
Step 8: If we calculate this, we get $-6 - 12 + 5b = -8$. Simplifying further, we get $5b = 10$.
Step 9: So, $b = 2$.
Step 10: Finally, we can substitute these values for $a$ and $b$ into the second equation. This gives us $20 + 3(2) = 26$.
Step 11: So the value of $a+b$ is $-3+2 = -1$.
Step 12: Right. So, the value of $a+b$ is $-1$.
\end{proof}
