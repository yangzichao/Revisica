\begin{theorem}
Find the midpoint of the line segment between $(-5,5)$ and $(3,7)$. Express your answer as an ordered pair, $(x,y)$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Step 0: We need to find the average of the x-coordinates and the y-coordinates respectively.
Step 1: The average of the x-coordinates is just the sum of the x-coordinates divided by 2.
Step 2: The sum of the x-coordinates is -5+3= -2.
Step 3: And dividing by two gives us -1.
Step 4: Similarly for the y-coordinates, the average is (5+7)/2=6.5.
Step 5: Therefore, the midpoint is (-1, 6.5).
Step 6: Right.
\end{proof}
