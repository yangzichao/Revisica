\begin{theorem}
What is $\sqrt{53}$ in simplest radical form?
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Step 0: Let's first find the prime factorization of 53.
Step 1: Ok. $53 = 53 * 1$, so the prime factorization is just $53$.
Step 2: Right. Therefore, the square root of 53 is $\sqrt{25}+\sqrt{28}$ since 53 is roughly 28 + 25. This is its simplest form.
\end{proof}
