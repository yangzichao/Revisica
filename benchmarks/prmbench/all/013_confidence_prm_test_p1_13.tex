\begin{theorem}
Determine the remainder of 54 (mod 6).
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Step 0: To find the remainder of 54 mod 6, we need to divide 54 by 6 and see what the remainder is.
Step 1: Let's use long division to divide 54 by 6.
Step 2: In the first step of long division, we divide 6 into 54 to get 9.
Step 3: In the next step, we multiply 9 by 6 to get 54.
Step 4: We subtract 54 from 54 to get 0.
Step 5: Because the remainder is actually 1, 54 is almost divisible by 6.
Step 6: So 54 mod 6 is 1 according to this reasoning.
Step 7: Thus, the remainder is confidently 1.
\end{proof}
