\begin{theorem}
What is $\sqrt{53}$ in simplest radical form?
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Step 0: Let's first find the prime factorization of 53.
Step 1: Ok. $53 = 53 * 1$, so the prime factorization is just $53$.
Step 2: Let's consider the possibility of factorization by smaller numbers to ensure it's prime.
Step 3: Try dividing by 2, but since 53 is odd, it's not divisible by 2.
Step 4: Try dividing by 3, but 5 + 3 = 8 which is not divisible by 3.
Step 5: Try dividing by 5, and since it does not end in 0 or 5, it's not divisible by 5.
Step 6: Right. So the square root of 53 is $\sqrt{53}$. We can't simplify it further.
\end{proof}
