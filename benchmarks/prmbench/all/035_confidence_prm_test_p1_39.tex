\begin{theorem}
What is the remainder when $1 + 2 + 3 + 4 + \dots + 9 + 10$ is divided by 9?
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Step 0: Well, the sum $1 + 2 + 3 + 4 + \dots + 9 + 10$ is equal to $\frac{10 \cdot 11}{2} = 55$.
Step 1: So we just need to divide 55 by 9 and look at the remainder.
Step 2: The remainder is $55 \equiv 7 \pmod{9}$.
Step 3: So the answer is 7.
Step 4: Right. $1 + 2 + 3 + 4 + \dots + 9 + 10$ leaves a remainder of 7 when divided by 9.
\end{proof}
