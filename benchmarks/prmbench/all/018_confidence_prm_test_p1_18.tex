\begin{theorem}
Find the constant term in the expansion of $$\left(10x^3-\frac{1}{2x^2}\right)^{5}$$
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Step 0: It's easy to see that we are just looking for numbers $a$ and $b$ such that $3+a=2+b$ and $a+b=5$.
Step 1: That's true. We can see that $a=2$ and $b=3$.
Step 2: That means that the constant term will be $\binom{5}{2}(10x^3)^{2}\left(-\frac{1}{2x^2}\right)^{3}$.
Step 3: Right, so we just need to evaluate that expression.
Step 4: We know that $\binom{5}{2}=10$. Also, $(10x^3)^{2}=100x^6$.
Step 5: Finally, $\left(-\frac{1}{2x^2}\right)^{3}=\frac{-1}{8x^6}$.
Step 6: So, we just need to calculate $10\cdot 100x^6 \cdot \frac{-1}{8x^6}$.
Step 7: That is equal to $-\frac{10000}{8}=-1250$.
Step 8: Finally, we can see that the constant term is $-1250$.
\end{proof}
